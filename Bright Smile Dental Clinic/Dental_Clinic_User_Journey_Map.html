🦷 New Patient Journey — "I have a toothache, now what?"

Mapping how a first-time patient goes from noticing tooth pain to becoming a returning, referring patient — used to prioritize what the new website needs to do at each step.

🙋‍♀️

Priya Sharma

34 · Marketing Manager · Jaipur

"I just want a dentist I can trust, booked in two minutes, without ten phone calls."
Goals
  • Get tooth pain treated quickly, by someone reliable
  • Avoid surprise costs
  • Book without calling during work hours
Frustrations
  • Mild dental anxiety — avoids the topic until it hurts
  • Can't tell which clinics are actually good online

Actions
  • Notices sharp tooth pain while eating
  • Ignores it for a day, hoping it fades
  • Googles "dentist near me Jaipur"
  • Skims Google Maps ratings
  • Opens 2–3 clinic websites in tabs
  • Compares services & reads testimonials
  • Clicks "Book Appointment"
  • Fills form, picks earliest slot
  • Arrives, completes intake form
  • Consultation, treatment, pays
  • Gets follow-up SMS
  • Leaves a Google review
Thoughts & Quotes
"Is this serious, or will it just go away?"
"Which of these is actually good, not just paid ads?"
"Do they even treat emergencies today? What's this going to cost?"
"I hope this actually confirms — I don't want to call and wait on hold."
"I hope this doesn't take forever or hurt too much."
"That was so much easier than I expected. I'd tell a friend."
Touchpoints
  • Own experience
  • Asks a friend on WhatsApp
  • Google Search
  • Google Business Profile / Maps
  • Clinic website — Services & Testimonials pages
  • Online booking form
  • Confirmation SMS / email
  • Front desk
  • Dentist & payment counter
  • Follow-up SMS/email
  • Google review prompt
Pain Points
Doesn't know any dentist nearby; anxious about cost & pain
Too many similar-looking options; some sites look outdated
No visible pricing; unclear if same-day slots exist
Booking form feels long; no instant confirmation
Wait time; re-fills a paper form she already submitted online
No reminder for next cleaning; nobody asks for a review in the moment
Website Opportunities
Emergency care messaging visible sitewide, not buried
Strong Google Business Profile; homepage trust signals (rating, years open)
Transparent starting prices; clear "same-day appointments available" note
Short 3-field booking form; instant SMS/email confirmation
Digital intake form pre-fills from the booking submission
Automated 6-month recall reminder; same-day review request
How to use this map: Each row is a lens the website design needs to satisfy at every stage — not just the Booking column. The biggest opportunity gap sits in Evaluation (Stage 3) and Booking (Stage 4): if pricing transparency and a short booking flow aren't solved there, Priya drops off to a competitor before ever reaching the clinic. This map feeds directly into the Sitemap and Homepage Wireframe already in review.
